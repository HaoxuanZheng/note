# 🟦 1. **The 80/20 Rule for Learning**
**20% of the content produces 80% of the results.**  
Identify the “high‑yield” topics and master those first.

For AP Precalculus, the 20% is:
- Polynomial/rational behavior  
- Exponential/log equations  
- Unit circle + sinusoidal modeling  
- Graph interpretation  
- Modeling FRQ structure  

**Technique:**  
Make a list of the *top 10 most tested skills* and drill only those until automatic.

---

# 🟦 2. **Active Recall (The #1 Learning Technique)**
Don’t reread. Don’t highlight.  
**Test yourself constantly.**

Examples:
- Close your notes → explain the concept out loud.  
- Solve a problem from memory without looking at steps.  
- Write down everything you know about a topic on a blank page.

**Why it works:**  
Your brain strengthens retrieval pathways, not reading pathways.

---

# 🟦 3. **Spaced Repetition (The #1 Memory Technique)**
Review material at increasing intervals:
- Day 1  
- Day 3  
- Day 7  
- Day 14  
- Day 30  

Use:
- Anki  
- Quizlet  
- Your own flashcards  

**Perfect for:**  
- Unit circle  
- Log rules  
- Trig identities  
- Function forms  
- Modeling steps  

---

# 🟦 4. **The Feynman Technique (Learn Anything Fast)**
1. Pick a concept  
2. Explain it like you’re teaching a 5th grader  
3. Identify gaps  
4. Simplify again  

If you can’t explain it simply, you don’t understand it deeply.

---

# 🟦 5. **Interleaving (Mixing Topics Boosts Mastery)**
Instead of studying one topic for 2 hours, mix 3–4 topics.

Example:
- 20 min polynomials  
- 20 min logs  
- 20 min trig  
- 20 min rational functions  

This forces your brain to **choose the right method**, not memorize patterns.

---

# 🟦 6. **Blurting Method (Fastest Way to Test Knowledge)**
Take a blank sheet and “blurt out” everything you know about a topic:
- Definitions  
- Formulas  
- Graph shapes  
- Steps  
- Examples  

Then compare to your notes.  
Repeat until you can blurt the entire topic from memory.

---

# 🟦 7. **The 5‑Minute Rule (Beat Procrastination)**
Tell yourself:
> “I’ll study for just 5 minutes.”

Your brain starts, momentum builds, and you end up studying 30–60 minutes.

---

# 🟦 8. **The 3‑Layer Problem‑Solving Method**
For math mastery:

### **Layer 1 — Imitation**
Follow worked examples.

### **Layer 2 — Reproduction**
Solve similar problems without looking.

### **Layer 3 — Creation**
Solve mixed problems where you must choose the method.

This is how you become exam‑proof.

# 🟦 9. **Error Analysis (The Secret Weapon of 5‑Scorers)**
Every mistake is a goldmine.

For each mistake:
- What did I do wrong?  
- Why did I do it?  
- How do I prevent it?  
- What rule or concept was missing?  

Make a “Mistake Log” — review it weekly.

- Tables  
- Color‑coded notes

Your brain remembers visuals far better than text.
